# Frameworkless Native Web

Frameworklessness is not nostalgia. It is a refusal to mistake accumulation for progress.

At its root is a simple intuition: one should not place a second world on top of the first unless the first has truly failed. If the native medium is already expressive enough, then another layer may increase motion while decreasing understanding.

## Main convictions

- A tool should clarify reality, not replace it.
- Abstraction is justified only when it removes essential difficulty rather than hiding it.
- Skill should accumulate in durable knowledge, not temporary dialects.
- Simplicity is not the absence of power; it is power that remains legible.

## Why this matters

A framework may speed the first week while burdening the fifth year. That trade is often accepted because short-term movement feels like intelligence. But long-lived systems are not saved by momentum. They are saved by clarity.

To stay close to the native medium is to keep the system closer to the truth of its own environment. Less translation means fewer illusions.

## The deeper principle

The medium should be trusted before it is escaped.

## What this rejects

- the assumption that productivity requires a framework
- the assumption that convenience is the same thing as simplicity
- the assumption that distance from the platform is a sign of maturity
